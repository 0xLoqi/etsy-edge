Etsy Tag Limit: 13 Tags Per Listing, 20 Characters Each

Published March 7, 2026

Etsy gives you 13 tags per listing, each up to 20 characters long. That is the Etsy tag limit, and it applies to every listing regardless of your shop tier or plan. The number has stayed at 13 for years and shows no sign of changing. If you are wondering how many tags on Etsy you should actually use, the answer is simple: all of them.

Most Sellers Leave Tags on the Table

Despite having 13 available slots, the average Etsy seller only uses 8 to 10 tags per listing. That means 3 to 5 empty tag slots on every listing -- each one a missed opportunity to appear in a buyer's search results.

Every tag you add is a chance to match a different search query. A buyer searching "ceramic coffee mug" and a buyer searching "handmade pottery gift" are looking for different things, but both might want your product. If you only have one of those phrases as a tag, you are invisible to the other buyer. With 13 tags, you get 13 chances to show up. With 8, you get 8. The math is not complicated.

Why Each Tag Slot Matters

Etsy's search algorithm uses tags during the query-matching phase. It scans your tags, title, categories, and attributes to decide whether your listing is relevant to a given search. Tags that are not there cannot match anything. An empty slot is not neutral -- it is a search query where your listing will never appear.

Think of your 13 tags as 13 doorways into your listing. The more doors you open, the more paths buyers can take to find you. Sellers who consistently fill all 13 slots cast a wider net across the searches happening in their category every day.

How to Fill All 13 Tags Strategically

Filling all 13 slots does not mean stuffing them with random words. Each tag should be a deliberate, multi-word phrase that matches how a real buyer would search. Here is a practical approach:

  • Use multi-word phrases, not single words. A tag like "earrings" puts you against millions of listings. A tag like "gold hoop earrings" is specific and matches real buyer intent. Use as much of the 20-character limit as you can.
  • Cover different angles. Include synonyms ("pottery cup" vs. "ceramic mug"), use cases ("coffee lover gift"), occasions ("housewarming present"), and style descriptors ("rustic farmhouse mug").
  • Do not duplicate your title exactly. Your title already carries strong SEO weight. If your title says "handmade ceramic coffee mug," you do not need that exact phrase as a tag. Use that slot for a variation the title does not cover, like "stoneware tea cup" or "artisan pottery gift."
  • Research what buyers actually search. Type your product type into Etsy's search bar and watch the autocomplete suggestions. Those are real queries from real shoppers. An Etsy tag tool can also reveal what tags top-ranking competitors are using, so you can fill your slots with proven phrases instead of guessing.

A Quick Tag Audit Checklist

Open any of your listings right now and check:

  1. Are all 13 tag slots filled?
  2. Is every tag a multi-word phrase (not a single word)?
  3. Does each tag target a distinct search query?
  4. Are you using buyer language, not seller jargon?
  5. Have you avoided repeating your exact title as a tag?

If you answered "no" to any of these, you have room to improve -- and it takes less than five minutes per listing.
