Etsy Title Character Limit: 140 Characters (What Top Sellers Actually Use)

The Etsy title character limit is 140 characters. That is the maximum length Etsy allows for any listing title. But hitting exactly 140 is not the goal -- using those characters strategically is. The sweet spot for most listings falls between 100 and 140 characters: long enough to include meaningful keyword variations, short enough to avoid sounding like gibberish.

Why 140 Characters Matters

Most sellers underuse their titles. The average Etsy listing title is around 60-80 characters, which means sellers are leaving half their title space -- and half their keyword potential -- on the table.

Your title is the single strongest ranking signal in Etsy search. Every word in it tells the algorithm what your listing is about and which searches it should appear in. A 70-character title can target maybe two or three keyword phrases. A well-crafted 130-character title can target five or six, dramatically expanding your search visibility without any additional cost or effort.

Front-Load Your Most Important Keywords

Etsy's search algorithm gives more weight to the words at the beginning of your title. This means your primary keyword -- the one search term you most want to rank for -- should appear in the first 40 characters.

This also matters for the buyer experience. On mobile and in search results, Etsy truncates long titles. Buyers will only see the first 40-50 characters before the title gets cut off. If your most compelling, descriptive words are buried at the end, shoppers scrolling through results will never see them.

A practical structure that works well:

  1. Characters 1-40: Your primary keyword and core product description. Example: "Handmade Ceramic Coffee Mug"
  2. Characters 41-90: Secondary keywords, material details, or style descriptors. Example: "Wheel Thrown Stoneware Tea Cup"
  3. Characters 91-140: Long-tail variations, gift-related phrases, or occasion keywords. Example: "Unique Housewarming Gift for Coffee Lover"

Use the Full Space for Long-Tail Keywords

The back half of your title is where long-tail keyword opportunities live. These are the specific, multi-word phrases that face less competition and often convert better because they match precise buyer intent.

For example, "mug" gets millions of results. But "handmade ceramic mug gift for dad" has far less competition and targets someone who is ready to buy. You have 140 characters -- use the extra space to include these variations that shorter titles simply cannot fit.

If you are not sure which long-tail keywords to target, an Etsy title generator can help you brainstorm variations based on what is actually ranking in your category.

Common Title Mistakes

Even with 140 characters to work with, there are a few traps to avoid:

  • Keyword stuffing. Cramming in every synonym you can think of makes your title unreadable. If a buyer cannot parse your title, they will not click -- and click-through rate affects your ranking too.
  • Repeating keywords. Writing "Coffee Mug Ceramic Mug Pottery Mug Handmade Mug" wastes characters. Etsy does not reward repetition. Each word should earn its place.
  • Using all caps or excessive punctuation. It looks spammy and does not help with search. Etsy's algorithm ignores capitalization and most punctuation anyway.
  • Wasting characters on your shop name. Your shop name already appears on the listing. Do not burn title space on it unless your brand name is something buyers actively search for.

How to Check Your Title Length

The Bottom Line

The Etsy title max characters is 140. Use as many of them as you can without sacrificing readability. Front-load your best keywords, fill the middle with descriptive variations, and use the tail end for long-tail phrases that broaden your search reach. A well-optimized title is the single fastest way to improve your Etsy search visibility -- and it takes less than five minutes to fix.
